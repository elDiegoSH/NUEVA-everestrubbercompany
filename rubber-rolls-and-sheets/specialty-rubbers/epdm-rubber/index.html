EPDM Rubber (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer)

EPDM Rubber (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer) is a synthetic elastomer recognized for its exceptional resistance to weather, ozone, severe environmental conditions and excellent low-temperature performance.

With a Shore A hardness of 60-65 points and a wide operating temperature range from -20°C to 145°C, EPDM is the preferred material for outdoor applications requiring durability and aging resistance.

Superior Resistance Properties

EPDM Rubber stands out for its unique properties for outdoor applications:

  • Excellent weather resistance and aging
  • Superior ozone resistance and UV radiation
  • High elongation (400% minimum) and flexibility
  • Excellent alcohol resistance and chemical products
  • Good low-temperature performance
  • Excellent vibration and impact absorption

These properties make it indispensable in construction, automotive, industrial applications, and any outdoor environment exposed to severe weather conditions.

Industries and applications where EPDM is the preferred choice

Construction

Automotive

Window seals, door gaskets, air conditioning system gaskets, and exterior components.

Industrial

Gaskets for outdoor equipment, conveyor belts, acoustic insulation, and weather-resistant components.

Electrical

Home

Advantages of EPDM Rubber

Excellent Weather Resistance

Superior resistance to UV, ozone, rain, and extreme weather conditions for over 30 years.

High Elongation and Flexibility

400% minimum elongation, providing excellent deformation capacity and recovery.

Wide Temperature Range

Operates from -20°C to 145°C, maintaining flexibility under extreme conditions.

Superior Ozone Resistance

No cracks after 72 hours of exposure to 200 PPHM ozone at 40°C.

Excellent Alcohol Resistance

Excellent performance with alcohols and good chemical resistance.

Damping Properties

Excellent vibration and impact absorption for dynamic applications.
